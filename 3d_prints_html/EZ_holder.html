Michael Vladimirsky

This holder was designed for a specific optical sensing application where positioning was absolutely critical. The goal was to secure an infrared LED and an aperture mask with zero vibration and perfect alignment. I designed the enclosure with a press-fit system that keeps the optical components perfectly centered within the housing. It was an exercise in precision light-masking—ensuring that light wouldn't leak around the edges of the aperture—and it helped me refine my design process for small-scale, high-accuracy sensor housings that need to be both robust and reproducible.
