Michael Vladimirsky

This car axle model was a deep dive into automotive assembly logic. My goal was to accurately represent how rotational torque is transferred from an input shaft to the wheel hub. I broke the assembly down into individual, modular components—the housing, the CV joints, and the axle shaft—to understand how they interlock and behave under load. Beyond just the modeling, this served as an excellent study in assembly alignment and clearances, ensuring that every part fits together with the precision required for a functional, moving system.
