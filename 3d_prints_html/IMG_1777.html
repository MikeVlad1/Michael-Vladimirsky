Michael Vladimirsky

Kashakas are incredibly fun to play, but it is difficult to find them in the specific size and weight ratio that I prefer for professional-grade playability. I decided to 3D print my own shells to experiment with different wall thicknesses and internal fill patterns. By adjusting the infill density, I was able to fine-tune the resonance and the weight of the balls, which completely changes the sound and the tactile 'feel' of the instrument. It’s a perfect example of how additive manufacturing allows for the rapid iteration of gear to meet highly specific personal requirements.
