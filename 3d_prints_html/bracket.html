Michael Vladimirsky

While working on a recent cabinetry project in my home shop, I realized that standard off-the-shelf brackets weren't cutting it for the specific load-bearing requirements I had. I designed these in SolidWorks, focusing on a geometry that minimizes material use while maximizing shear strength. I iterated through two distinct versions: the first was a rapid prototype for fitment testing, and the second was optimized for FDM printing with reinforced ribbing. This taught me a great deal about managing the trade-offs between print time and structural performance, ultimately leading to a more reliable installation.
