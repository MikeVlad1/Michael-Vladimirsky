Michael Vladimirsky

Reaching the manual crank on my skylight was a constant frustration, so I decided to design a custom interface to bridge that gap. This project was a study in ergonomics and torque; I had to calculate the mechanical advantage required to turn the window smoothly without stripping the gear teeth. I ended up designing a handle that fits comfortably in the palm, providing just enough leverage to operate the mechanism with one hand. It is a simple, highly functional piece, but it serves as a great example of how 3D printing can be used to quickly engineer solutions for everyday mechanical annoyances.
